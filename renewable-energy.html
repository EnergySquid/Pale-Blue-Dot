What is renewable energy? Renewable energy refers to any source of energy that is not deprived when used. The sun doesn't shine any less when you put out a solar panel. The wind is just as strong when there's a wind turbine. There are many different sources of renewable energy, but they all fall into 4 main categories. Solar, wind, hydro, and geothermal. The most common, solar, contains more than just solar panels. In large operations, mirrors are used to direct sunlight onto a pipe full of water, this boils the water and the resulting steam is then used to power a turbine. This runs on many of the same principles as fossil fuel plants, except that the heat comes from the sun, not the burning of fossil fuels. Hydroelectric power is generated by forcing flowing water over a turbine, as seen in the image to the right. Geothermal energy is probably one of the least commonly known forms of renewable energy. It uses the Earth's natural heat to generate electricity. Geologically active places are often where geothermal plants are placed. They typically use volcanic heat to generate electricity.

Renewable energy is going to be a very important part of our future. Renewable energy is cheaper than traditional forms of electrical energy generation, and has no negative effects on our planet. Household solar panels will actually save a person usually over $20,000 over 20 years, including the original cost of the panels and the cost of maintaining them.
On the non-individual level, companies could switch to even more efficient and larger scale renewable energy sources, and governments could switch their electrical grids to more and more renewable sources, each of which pays for itself in the long run. Switching to renewable energy would create lots of construction jobs in the process.
If you've decided that you want to reduce your carbon footprint, there are a multitude of things you can do. You could go all out and make a large investment in solar panels for your home. If that's out of the budget or your house isn't compatible, you can do some simple things on a daily basis, like turning off any unneeded lights when you leave a room, turning off desktop computers while not in use (not just putting them to sleep), and unplugging any unused appliances.
